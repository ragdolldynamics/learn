    Limitations

    Before we get too excited, let's talk about what this can't do. This technology generates locomotion from scratch, given:

    1. A starting position
    2. An ending position
    3. A "step sequence"

    However, it only understands 2 things.

    1. The body
    2. The foot

    And for feet, it only understand the position of the foot, not its orientation.

    Most importantly, it does not understand arms! Arms are critical to human locomotion, they swing in tandem with each step. This version of Ragdoll does not understand arms. Yet. Meaning it's good for locomotion involving any creature that does not have arms.

    But Marcus, that doesn't leave much room for many creatures. They all have arms!

    Think again!

    • Dogs
    • Cats
    • ..any quadruped!
    • 6-legged creatures, e.g. crabs
    • 8-legged creatures, e.g. spiders
    • n-legged tentacle monsters

    The legs themselves aren't taken into consideration. Which might seem like it's not enough information to walk?! But, surprisingly, with only these 2 concepts, we'll be able achieve a large variety of locomotion.

    Let's put it this way, out of every possible locomotion, Ragdoll is currently able to automate just a tiny tiny portion of it.


    Beta

    One last thing about limitations, but more related to this particular Beta, is that out of all possible combinations of input you can give to Ragdoll, only a tiny tiny portion of that is actually "valid". For example, you can give Ragdoll an infinite mass, but Ragdoll cannot work with infinite masses. You can ask it to put a foot 3 kilometers away from the body, but it cannot find any reasonable solution to that request.

    So part of the challenge of this beta is finding the boundaries of what it actually can do, and that's where we could use your help!

    Now, with that out the way, let's look at what it can do!


    Abilities

    Here's what we're aiming for with this release.

    https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k7cGyYaxUnI&t=11s&ab_channel=BBCEarth

    To achieve this, you've got control over:

    1. The start and end positions of the body and feet
    2. The order and duration of steps, called a Step Sequence
    3. An optional Terrain upon which to walk

    There can be any number of feet and it can travel any amount of distance. The Step Sequence is how you're able to achieve different kind of walks.

    • Walking
    • Running
    • Trotting
    • Dancing
    • ...

    And the Terrain is how it can do this across geometry of any complexity.

    Can you combine this with Markers? Yes!


    Workflow

    Here's what you do.

    1. Select body
    2. Select feet
    3. Go!

    And you can have multiple characters in the same scene too.
